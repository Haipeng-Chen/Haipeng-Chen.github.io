Haipeng Chen

Email: hpchen@seas.harvard.edu; haipengkeepon@gmail.com

I study AI techniques that help understand the world and benefit the society. From a technical perspective, I am interested in predictive decision making, reinforcement learning, learning on graph/network-structured data, and generative models. From an application perspective, I focus on domains such as public health, cybersecurity, transportation, and online/real-world social networks. I am actively collaborating or have collaborated with amazing folks from non-profits such as The Family Van, Mobile Health Map, Safe Place for Youth. My past work has been covered by media such as The Wall Street Journal, Scientific American, Digital Guardian, ScienceBlog, AAAS and EurekAlert!, and 雷锋网.

Short bio: I am a CRCS postdoctoral fellow at Harvard University from July 2020, where I work with Professor Milind Tambe . Previously I did my first postdoc in the Department of Computer Science, Dartmouth College from July 2018, where I worked with Professor V.S. Subrahmanian. I obtained my PhD from Interdisciplinary Graduate School (IGS), Nanyang Technological University (NTU) in 2018, where I was advised by Professor Bo An. I got my B.S. in Physics from University of Science and Technology of China (USTC) in 2013.
